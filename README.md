Product Details 
iRobot Roomba
The iRobot Roomba is an autonomous robotic vacuum cleaner designed to automatically clean floors without human intervention. It is one of the most successful consumer robotics products in the world.
It was developed and manufactured by iRobot, a company that specializes in designing intelligent robotic systems for practical applications.
The Roomba is a small circular robotic device equipped with sensors, motors, and onboard computing systems that allow it to move around a room and clean the floor automatically. Unlike traditional vacuum cleaners, the Roomba does not require a person to guide it manually. Instead, it can navigate independently through the environment, detect obstacles, adjust its cleaning path, and return to its charging station when its battery is low.
This ability to sense its environment and make decisions makes it an intelligent robotic system.

Key Intelligent Features
1. Autonomous Navigation
The Roomba is capable of moving around rooms without any manual control. It continuously scans its surroundings using sensors to determine where it should move next.
2. Obstacle Detection and Avoidance
The robot is equipped with infrared sensors and bump sensors that help it detect objects such as walls, furniture, and other obstacles.


3. Cliff Detection Technology
One of the important safety features of the Roomba is the ability to detect stairs or sudden drops. Special sensors located on the underside of the robot detect changes in floor height and prevent the robot from falling.
4. Dirt Detection System
The robot can identify areas that contain a higher concentration of dirt or debris.
When dirt is detected, the robot automatically spends more time cleaning that specific area to ensure effective cleaning.
5. Automatic Docking and Charging
When the battery level becomes low, the Roomba automatically searches for its charging dock and connects to it.
6. Smart Connectivity
Modern Roomba models can connect to smartphones through a mobile application.
Users can:
•	schedule cleaning sessions
•	start or stop cleaning remotely
•	monitor cleaning progress
Some models can also integrate with smart home assistants.

Data Used by the Robot
The robot collects various types of data from sensors.
Data Type	Purpose
Distance data	Detect obstacles
Cliff detection data	Prevent falling from stairs

Battery data	Manage charging

Working Principle
The robot vacuum works through the following process:
1.	Sensors scan the surrounding environment.
2.	The onboard processor analyzes sensor data.
3.	Navigation algorithms determine the cleaning path.
4.	Motors move the robot across the floor.
5.	Brushes and suction system collect dust.
6.	When battery becomes low, the robot returns to its charging dock.
